## 300 K Constant-Temperature Hold

After completing the 50 K → 300 K thermal ramp, a short NVT hold was performed at 300 K to test whether the hydrated BN-like tubular scaffold remains stable under constant room-temperature conditions.

The hold used the final 300 K ramp configuration as input and ran for 10,000 additional LAMMPS steps with a 0.25 fs timestep.

Final hold metrics:

- Final water temperature: 299.74 K
- Final global temperature: 251.46 K
- Final water MSD: 12.349 Å²
- Dangerous builds: 0
- No NaNs detected
- No lost atoms reported
- Output structure: `systems/inorganic/bn_like_scaffold_water/outputs/bn_like_scaffold_water_30000w_nvt300_hold.data`
- Output trajectory: `systems/inorganic/bn_like_scaffold_water/outputs/nvt300_hold_30000w.lammpstrj`

Interpretation: the system remains numerically stable during a short 300 K constant-temperature hold. The water-specific temperature remains centered around 300 K, and the MSD continues to increase, indicating continued molecular mobility under confinement. These results strengthen the Phase 0 conclusion that the current BN-like scaffold-water setup is suitable for workflow validation and short-timescale stability testing. The force-field remains provisional and should not yet be interpreted as a final physical material model.

## 300 K Hold Confinement Check

A confinement analysis was performed on the 300 K NVT hold trajectory using the nominal lumen radius of 70 Å, outer scaffold radius of 120 Å, and half-length of 100 Å.

Final 300 K hold confinement metrics:

- Fraction of water oxygen atoms inside the nominal lumen segment: 0.9983
- Fraction of water oxygen atoms radially outside the lumen radius: 0.00163
- Fraction of water oxygen atoms axially outside the nominal segment: 0.000133
- Fraction of water oxygen atoms outside the outer scaffold radius: 0.0
- Mean water oxygen radius at final frame: 42.43 Å
- Mean |z| at final frame: 41.44 Å

Interpretation: after the 300 K thermal ramp and an additional 300 K hold, the water remains overwhelmingly confined inside the nominal lumen. The small radial excursions above the nominal 70 Å lumen radius are minor relative to the total water population and no water oxygen atoms cross the outer scaffold radius. This supports the use of the current fixed-scaffold setup as a short-timescale Phase 0 stability and workflow-validation baseline.

## Extended Contained 300 K Hold

An additional 300 K extended contained hold was performed after the short 300 K hold. This run was introduced after an open-boundary extended hold failed due to one water molecule reaching the axial edge of the finite simulation box and causing a missing bonded-atom event. Inspection showed that the affected water molecule had valid topology but was located near the lower axial boundary.

The corrective contained protocol used:

- reflective finite-box boundaries through `fix wall/reflect`;
- rigid water geometry through `fix shake`;
- reduced timestep of 0.10 fs;
- fixed scaffold atoms;
- water-specific NVT thermostat at 300 K.

The extended contained run completed successfully:

- Runtime: 20,000 steps
- Timestep: 0.10 fs
- Simulated time: 2.0 ps
- Final water temperature: 300.06 K
- Final water MSD: 7.866 Å²
- Dangerous builds: 0
- No NaNs detected
- No lost atoms reported
- No missing bonded atoms reported

Interpretation:

The previous open-boundary extended hold failure should be interpreted as a finite-box boundary artifact rather than as an invalid molecular topology. The contained extended 300 K run demonstrates that the 30,000-water BN-like scaffold system can remain numerically stable for a longer room-temperature test when the finite simulation domain is treated consistently as a confined system. This remains a Phase 0 workflow-validation and numerical-stability result; the force field is still provisional and the contained protocol is not directly equivalent to the earlier flexible-water open-boundary hold.

## Post-Transient Interpretation of the Extended Contained Hold

Because the extended contained protocol introduced SHAKE-constrained water and reflective finite-box containment after the previous flexible-water hold, the initial thermo record of the extended run contains a startup transient. The scientifically relevant stability interpretation should therefore use the post-transient portion of the extended contained trajectory, not the initial step alone.

A conservative post-transient window was defined as:

- `timestep >= 3500`
- corresponding to approximately 0.35 ps onward for the 0.10 fs extended-contained timestep

Post-transient summary tables were generated for:

- thermo stability metrics;
- confinement metrics.

These post-transient summaries should be used for reporting the extended contained 300 K stability behavior.
