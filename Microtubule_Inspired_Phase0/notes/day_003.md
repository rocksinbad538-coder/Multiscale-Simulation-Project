## 300 K Constant-Temperature Hold

After completing the 50 K → 300 K thermal ramp, a short NVT hold was performed at 300 K to test whether the hydrated BN-like tubular scaffold remains stable under constant room-temperature conditions.

The hold used the final 300 K ramp configuration as input and ran for 10,000 additional LAMMPS steps with a 0.25 fs timestep.

Final hold metrics:

- Final water temperature: 299.74 K
- Final global temperature: 251.46 K
- Final water MSD: 12.349 Å²
- Dangerous builds: 0
- No NaNs detected
- No lost atoms reported
- Output structure: `systems/inorganic/bn_like_scaffold_water/outputs/bn_like_scaffold_water_30000w_nvt300_hold.data`
- Output trajectory: `systems/inorganic/bn_like_scaffold_water/outputs/nvt300_hold_30000w.lammpstrj`

Interpretation: the system remains numerically stable during a short 300 K constant-temperature hold. The water-specific temperature remains centered around 300 K, and the MSD continues to increase, indicating continued molecular mobility under confinement. These results strengthen the Phase 0 conclusion that the current BN-like scaffold-water setup is suitable for workflow validation and short-timescale stability testing. The force-field remains provisional and should not yet be interpreted as a final physical material model.

## 300 K Hold Confinement Check

A confinement analysis was performed on the 300 K NVT hold trajectory using the nominal lumen radius of 70 Å, outer scaffold radius of 120 Å, and half-length of 100 Å.

Final 300 K hold confinement metrics:

- Fraction of water oxygen atoms inside the nominal lumen segment: 0.9983
- Fraction of water oxygen atoms radially outside the lumen radius: 0.00163
- Fraction of water oxygen atoms axially outside the nominal segment: 0.000133
- Fraction of water oxygen atoms outside the outer scaffold radius: 0.0
- Mean water oxygen radius at final frame: 42.43 Å
- Mean |z| at final frame: 41.44 Å

Interpretation: after the 300 K thermal ramp and an additional 300 K hold, the water remains overwhelmingly confined inside the nominal lumen. The small radial excursions above the nominal 70 Å lumen radius are minor relative to the total water population and no water oxygen atoms cross the outer scaffold radius. This supports the use of the current fixed-scaffold setup as a short-timescale Phase 0 stability and workflow-validation baseline.

Key interpretation:

- The radial water-oxygen distribution remains concentrated inside the lumen, with only a small tail near and slightly above the nominal 70 Å lumen radius.
- The volume-corrected radial number-density profile was added because the raw radial fraction-per-bin profile is not a true cylindrical density; wider radial shells have larger volumes and must be normalized accordingly.

Caveat:

The radial and axial distributions are still short-timescale diagnostics. They should be treated as workflow-validation evidence, not as converged equilibrium density profiles.
