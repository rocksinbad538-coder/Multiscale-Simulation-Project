# Day 007 — First hybrid/chromophore-bearing BN-like scaffold-water embodiment

## Objective

Day 007 starts the transition from the validated BN-like polar scaffold-water baseline toward a lead hybrid/chromophore-bearing Phase 0 embodiment.

The aim is to preserve the stable BN-like polar confinement behavior while adding sparse electronically active sites that can later support excitonic, dipolar, or coherence-related modeling.

## Lead embodiment concept

Lead preliminary embodiment:

- BN-like polar tubular scaffold
- confined water
- sparse anchored chromophore-like dipolar sites on or near the inner wall
- scaffold and active sites fixed during the initial stability test
- water remains mobile and thermostatted at 300 K

## Back-up embodiment concept

Back-up preliminary embodiment:

- BN-like polar tubular scaffold
- confined water
- simplified neutral or weakly polar organic linker sites
- same geometry and site positions as the lead model, but reduced electrostatic perturbation

## Day 007 simulation plan

1. Start from the validated BN-like polar 50k field-free state.
2. Add sparse chromophore-like dipolar sites near the inner lumen wall.
3. Run an initial short 300 K contained MD stability test.
4. Compare water confinement against the BN-like polar baseline.
5. Decide whether to extend the hybrid model or reduce/modify active-site density.

## Initial acceptance criteria

- no NaNs, lost atoms, or missing bonds
- dangerous builds = 0
- maximum radial water oxygen position remains near the BN-like baseline
- fraction inside nominal lumen segment remains high
- no immediate destabilization of confined water

## First carved hybrid/chromophore-bearing model completed

The initial uncarved hybrid model produced severe overlap artifacts between newly inserted chromophore pseudo-sites and existing confined water molecules. This was detected by an extremely large initial potential energy and immediate lost atoms.

The model was therefore rebuilt using a carved geometry:

- starting structure: validated BN-like polar scaffold-water 50k field-free state
- chromophore model: 12 fixed dipolar pseudo-site pairs
- chromophore pseudo-atoms added: 24
- charge magnitude: +/- 0.10 e
- local water exclusion radius around chromophore pseudo-sites: 4.5 Å
- water molecules removed: 13
- final system size: 107,265 atoms
- water atoms: 89,961
- water molecules: 29,987
- scaffold atoms: 17,280
- chromophore pseudo-atoms: 24

The carved hybrid model completed a 5k-step contained 300 K stability test with:

- timestep: 0.05 fs
- final water temperature: approximately 300.43 K
- final potential energy: approximately -255,725 kcal/mol
- final pressure: approximately -71.99
- dangerous builds: 0
- no lost atoms after carving
- no NaNs or missing bonds detected

Interpretation:

The first sparse chromophore-bearing BN-like hybrid model is mechanically and thermally stable over the initial 5k-step screening test after local water carving. This provides the first viable lead hybrid embodiment candidate for subsequent Phase 0 extension and later Phase 1 geometry extraction.
