# Day 006 — Longer BN-like polar scaffold-water stability extension

## Objective

Day 006 starts from the Day 005 conclusion that the BN-like polar scaffold-water system is the strongest current Phase 0 inorganic baseline. The goal is to move from short charge/polarity screening diagnostics toward longer stability validation.

The first Day 006 simulation target is to extend the BN-like polar scaffold-water contained 300 K trajectory from 20,000 total steps to 50,000 total steps.

## Rationale

Day 005 showed that the BN-like polar scaffold preserves radial water confinement both without field and under fieldZ, whereas BN-neutralized and carbon-like neutral matched controls lose radial confinement. Day 006 tests whether the BN-like polar confinement remains stable over a longer trajectory.

## Planned metrics

- water mean radial position
- maximum radial water oxygen position
- fraction inside lumen segment
- radial outside-lumen fraction
- axial outside-segment fraction
- water dipole orientation without applied field
- comparison against Day 005 20k values

The field-free water dipole orientation remains approximately isotropic, as expected in the absence of applied field.

## Day 006 stability comparison figure

A Day 006 comparison figure was generated to place the BN-like polar 50k field-free extension against the Day 005 matched controls.

Figure:

- `figures/day006/day006_bn_like_50k_stability_vs_day005_controls.png`

The figure confirms that the BN-like polar scaffold-water system maintains maximum radial water oxygen position near 73 Å through 50k steps, while BN-neutralized, carbon-like neutral, and water-only controls show substantially larger radial expansion over the shorter 20k screening interval.

This supports the Day 005 conclusion that BN-like wall polarity/charge patterning is a key confinement variable, and extends that conclusion to a longer field-free BN-like trajectory.

## Day 006 fieldZ extension comparison figure

A Day 006 fieldZ response figure was generated:

- `figures/day006/day006_bn_like_fieldZ_30k_response.png`

This figure compares the original BN-like polar fieldZ diagnostic from 0-10k steps against the Day 006 fieldZ extension from 10k-30k steps.

Main observations:

- Maximum radial water oxygen position remains confined near 73-74 Å through 30k fieldZ steps.
- Fraction inside the nominal lumen segment remains high, close to 0.996 at 30k.
- Axial water-dipole projection increases progressively under prolonged field exposure.
- The axial dipolar order parameter S_z also increases, reaching approximately 0.47 at 30k.

Interpretation:

The BN-like polar scaffold-water system preserves radial confinement under prolonged axial field exposure while allowing stronger axial water-dipole ordering. This strengthens the Day 005 conclusion that BN-like wall polarity/charge supports radial confinement, and extends it by showing that field-induced dipole ordering can develop without radial destabilization over the tested fieldZ timescale.
