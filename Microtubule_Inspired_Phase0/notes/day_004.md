# Day 004 — Dry Scaffold Control and Extended 300 K Stability Follow-up

## Context

Day 004 continues the Phase 0 inorganic BN-like baseline evaluation. The previous hydrated BN-like scaffold-water system completed the 50 K to 300 K thermal ramp, a short 300 K hold, and an extended contained 300 K hold. The next control is the dry/empty scaffold, intended to separate scaffold-only geometric stability from confined-water behavior.

## Water Dipole Orientation Analysis

A vectorized water-dipole orientation analysis was performed for both the hydrated BN-like scaffold-water system and the water-only contained control.

The water dipole direction was approximated from the oxygen atom toward the midpoint of the two hydrogen atoms in each water molecule. The axial orientational order parameter was computed as:

\[
S_z = \left\langle \frac{3\cos^2\theta_z - 1}{2} \right\rangle
\]

where \(S_z = 0\) indicates an isotropic orientation distribution relative to the tube axis, \(S_z > 0\) indicates preferential axial alignment, and \(S_z < 0\) indicates preferential orientation perpendicular to the tube axis.

Interpretation:

Both the hydrated scaffold-water system and the water-only contained control show axial dipolar order parameters close to zero. Therefore, within the current short 2.0 ps contained trajectories and provisional force-field model, there is no evidence of strong axial water-dipole ordering.

This result is scientifically useful because it separates two effects:

- the BN-like scaffold contributes to radial confinement of the water distribution;
- the same short-timescale data do not support a strong scaffold-induced axial dipolar alignment.

This distinction should be preserved in the Phase 0 interpretation. The current result is a short-timescale diagnostic and should not be interpreted as a converged dielectric or orientational equilibrium property.

## Water Dipole Orientation Control Comparison

Water dipole orientation was compared between the hydrated BN-like scaffold-water system and the water-only contained control.

The analysis used the water molecular dipole vector reconstructed from each oxygen atom and its two bonded hydrogen atoms. Dipole orientation was evaluated relative to the axial z direction and to the local radial direction.

Key interpretation:

- The axial dipolar order parameter remains close to zero in both systems.
- The final cos(theta_z) distributions are approximately uniform.
- The mean absolute axial projection remains close to the isotropic value of 0.5.
- The mean absolute radial projection also remains close to the isotropic value of 0.5.

Therefore, over this short 2.0 ps contained trajectory, neither the hydrated BN-like scaffold-water system nor the water-only contained control shows strong persistent water dipole alignment. The BN-like scaffold contributes more clearly to radial confinement than to axial dipolar ordering under the current provisional force-field and short-timescale conditions.

## Water Dipole Autocorrelation Comparison

Water dipole orientational autocorrelation was computed for the hydrated BN-like scaffold-water system and for the water-only contained control.

The analyzed quantity was:

C_mu(t) = < u_mu(0) · u_mu(t) >

where u_mu is the unit vector from the oxygen atom toward the midpoint of the two bonded hydrogen atoms.

Key results:

| Lag time | Hydrated scaffold-water C_mu | Water-only contained C_mu |
|---:|---:|---:|
| 0.1 ps | 0.8672 | 0.8629 |
| 1.0 ps | 0.6941 | 0.6855 |
| 2.0 ps | 0.5853 | 0.5732 |

Interpretation:

Both systems retain substantial water dipole orientational memory over the 2.0 ps contained trajectory. The hydrated BN-like scaffold-water system shows slightly slower orientational decorrelation than the water-only contained control. This suggests a weak scaffold-associated restriction of water rotational dynamics, although the effect is small over the current short trajectory.

This result complements the dipolar-order analysis. The water dipoles do not show strong persistent axial alignment, but they do retain orientational memory over the short trajectory. Therefore, the current BN-like scaffold appears to influence radial confinement more strongly than average dipolar alignment, with a weaker preliminary effect on orientational decorrelation.

## Axial Electric-Field Response Diagnostic

A short axial electric-field diagnostic was performed for both:

- hydrated BN-like scaffold-water system;
- water-only contained reference.

Both simulations used the same contained finite-domain protocol, SHAKE-constrained water, and a 300 K water thermostat. The applied axial field was:

- Ez = 0.01 V/Å
- LAMMPS real-units equivalent: 0.230605 kcal/(mol Å e)

Both field-response runs completed successfully:

| Case | Atoms | Runtime | Timestep | Simulated time | Dangerous builds | Errors |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Scaffold-water + fieldZ | 107,280 | 10,000 steps | 0.10 fs | 1.0 ps | 0 | none detected |
| Water-only + fieldZ | 90,000 | 10,000 steps | 0.10 fs | 1.0 ps | 0 | none detected |

Key observations:

- The axial electric field induced strong positive axial dipolar alignment in both systems.
- The axial dipolar order parameter increased from approximately zero to about 0.25–0.27 over 1.0 ps.
- The mean axial dipole projection increased to approximately 0.58–0.60.
- The final cos(theta_z) distributions shifted strongly toward +1, confirming field-induced water dipole alignment.
- The scaffold-water and water-only systems showed very similar field-induced dipolar response.
- The main difference remained radial confinement: under fieldZ, the water-only system developed a much larger radial tail than the scaffold-water system.

Final confinement comparison under fieldZ:

| Metric at 10,000 steps | Scaffold-water + fieldZ | Water-only + fieldZ |
|---|---:|---:|
| Mean water radial position | 41.50 Å | 40.36 Å |
| Maximum water radial oxygen position | 72.82 Å | 97.15 Å |
| Fraction inside nominal lumen segment | 0.9969 | 0.9975 |
| Fraction radially outside 70 Å | 0.0031 | 0.0024 |
| Fraction outside 120 Å outer radius | 0.0 | 0.0 |

Interpretation:

The BN-like scaffold preserves radial confinement under an applied axial electric field while allowing strong field-induced axial water-dipole alignment. Under the current provisional force field and short-timescale diagnostic, the scaffold does not strongly suppress axial dipole reorientation relative to the water-only contained reference. Its clearest contribution remains geometric/radial confinement of the water phase.
