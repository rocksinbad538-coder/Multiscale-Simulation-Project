# Day 011
## Date
2026-06-18

## Objective

Complete Phase 1A scaffold-mechanics validation and determine the accepted atomistic h-BN scaffold model before proceeding to hydration and electronic-structure stages.

---

## Work performed

### 1. Improper stiffness sensitivity study

Evaluated three geometry-referenced improper stiffness values:

- improperGeo100
- improperGeo150
- improperGeo200

Each model was subjected to:

- energy minimization
- NVT validation at 100 K
- PYR-HBN contact audits
- radial deformation audits

---

### 2. Comparative evaluation

Observed behavior:

#### improperGeo100

- stable nanotube geometry
- no contacts below 0.30 nm
- lowest overall deformation
- best global behavior

#### improperGeo150

- increased local PYR-HBN adsorption
- larger deformation excursions
- no improvement relative to improperGeo100

#### improperGeo200

- stronger local distortions
- larger contact populations
- no improvement relative to improperGeo100

Conclusion:

improperGeo100 provided the best balance between flexibility and geometric stability.

---

### 3. Extended validation trajectory

Performed progressively longer validation runs:

- 5 ps
- 10 ps
- 50 ps
- 100 ps

Conditions:

- NVT
- 100 K
- bonded h-BN scaffold mobile
- PYR frozen
- water frozen

Purpose:

isolate scaffold mechanics independently from chromophore and solvent dynamics.

---

### 4. Final 100 ps validation

Final PYR-HBN minimum distances:

- PYR2: 0.38235 nm
- PYR3: 0.32135 nm
- PYR4: 0.42663 nm
- PYR5: 0.70072 nm

No PYR-HBN contacts below 0.30 nm were observed.

Observed close contacts are interpreted as localized adsorption-like interactions rather than nanotube collapse.

---

### 5. Radial deformation audit

100 ps results:

- min Δr = -0.17150 nm
- max Δr = +0.23384 nm
- mean Δr = +0.01277 nm

No evidence of global radial collapse was observed.

---

### 6. Energy stability audit

Potential energy:

- average = -905721 kJ/mol
- RMSD = 73.37 kJ/mol
- total drift = 39.03 kJ/mol

Relative drift:

approximately 4.3×10^-5 of the absolute potential energy.

The trajectory is considered mechanically stable.

---

## Acceptance decision

Accepted scaffold-mechanics baseline:

- bonded h-BN nanotube
- kang2000 angular model
- improperGeo100 geometry-referenced improper model

Validation:

- NVT
- 100 ps
- 100 K

Outcome:

- no collapse
- no contacts below 0.30 nm
- stable energy
- stable deformation
