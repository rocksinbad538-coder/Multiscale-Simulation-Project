# Day 010 — Phase 1A Hydration Stability Audit

## Date

2026-06-17

## Objective

Complete the Phase 1A hydration stability audit and determine whether the observed instabilities originate from water packing, pyrene placement, h-BN scaffold mechanics, or the current dummy force-field representation.

## Work Completed

A systematic sequence of GROMACS tests was completed for the hydrated h-BN + 4 pyrene + TIP4P/2005 system.

The following cases were evaluated:

1. Standard NVT with mobile h-BN, mobile pyrenes, and mobile water.
2. NVT without global H-bond constraints.
3. NVT with strong pyrene position restraints.
4. NVT with frozen h-BN and frozen pyrenes, allowing only water to move.

## Key Findings

The fully mobile hydrated system remains unstable.

The instability does not originate from water packing or from the TIP4P/2005 hydration protocol. Instead, the instability is associated with the current dummy h-BN scaffold topology/mechanics when the scaffold is allowed to move.

The frozen-solute protocol, where h-BN and pyrenes are frozen and only water is mobile, is stable.

## Accepted 100 ps Frozen-Solute NVT Result

A 100 ps NVT simulation was completed successfully using:

- h-BN scaffold frozen
- all 4 pyrene chromophores frozen
- TIP4P/2005 water mobile
- dt = 0.0005 ps
- total simulation time = 100 ps
- PME electrostatics
- cutoff = 1.2 nm

## Final Structural Metrics

Minimum pyrene–hBN distances after 100 ps:

- PYR2: 0.44153 nm
- PYR3: 0.44466 nm
- PYR4: 0.44362 nm
- PYR5: 0.56126 nm

Final hydration contacts:

- min OW–solute distance = 0.24549 nm
- min water atom–solute distance = 0.18479 nm

## Interpretation

The hydration shell is stable and physically acceptable under the frozen-solute protocol.

The current Phase 1A dummy h-BN scaffold is not suitable for unconstrained/mobile molecular dynamics because its mechanical/topological representation is not chemically physical.

The accepted 100 ps frozen-solute trajectory is retained as the hydration-validation baseline for Phase 1A.
