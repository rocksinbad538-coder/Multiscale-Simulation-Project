# Day 002 — Phase 0 Inorganic Baseline: BN-like Scaffold + Confined Water

## Plan

Build and scale the Phase 0 inorganic baseline simulation system by generating BN-like tubular scaffold + confined-water LAMMPS models at increasing hydration levels, then run LAMMPS read/initialization tests to verify atom counts, topology, neighbor lists, and file consistency. After identifying a locally tractable system size, proceed with relaxation, minimization, and short finite-temperature NVT tests.

## Results

A BN-like inorganic tubular scaffold with confined water was successfully generated, initialized, relaxed, minimized, and tested under finite-temperature NVT conditions.

The main working system for local testing contains:

- BN-like scaffold atoms: 17,280
- Confined water molecules: 30,000
- Water atoms: 90,000
- Total atoms: 107,280
- Bonds: 60,000
- Angles: 30,000

Additional scaling tests were also performed:

- 2,000 waters / 23,280 total atoms: LAMMPS read test passed.
- 10,000 waters / 47,280 total atoms: LAMMPS read test passed.
- 30,000 waters / 107,280 total atoms: LAMMPS read test passed and used for relaxation/NVT testing.
- 60,000 waters / 197,280 total atoms: lattice-based generation and LAMMPS read test passed.

The original random water insertion strategy became inefficient at high hydration levels and was replaced by a lattice-based confined-water generator. This was an important technical correction because random sequential insertion becomes prohibitively slow as packing density increases.

For the 30,000-water system, the following simulation sequence was completed:

1. Initial minimization.
2. Second minimization.
3. Controlled low-temperature dynamic relaxation.
4. Post-relaxation FIRE minimization.
5. Corrected NVT test at 50 K.
6. NVT temperature ramp from 50 K to 150 K.
7. NVT temperature ramp from 150 K to 200 K.

The corrected temperature reporting confirmed that the relevant water-group temperature, `c_twater`, was controlled properly. The global temperature is lower because the scaffold is fixed and included in the global default temperature.

Key numerical observations:

- 50 K corrected NVT:
  - Water temperature remained close to 50 K.
  - No NaNs.
  - No lost atoms.
  - No dangerous neighbor builds.
  - Water MSD increased smoothly.

- 150 K NVT ramp:
  - Water temperature increased from approximately 50 K to approximately 149 K.
  - No NaNs.
  - No lost atoms.
  - No dangerous neighbor builds.
  - Water MSD increased smoothly to approximately 1.89 A^2.

- 200 K NVT ramp:
  - Water temperature increased from approximately 149 K to approximately 200 K.
  - No NaNs.
  - No lost atoms.
  - No dangerous neighbor builds.
  - Water MSD increased smoothly to approximately 4.09 A^2.

A VMD screenshot was taken as visual evidence of the scaffold-water system. The trajectory and generated visualization-ready files are available locally for further visual inspection, video generation, and GitHub documentation.

## Technical Notes

The local Mac environment is suitable for:

- geometry generation;
- LAMMPS input preparation;
- read/initialization tests;
- short relaxation and NVT tests;
- visualization preparation.

However, performance is limited for production-scale MD. The 107,280-atom system runs at approximately 0.11–0.12 ns/day locally for the current settings. Longer trajectories, multiple temperatures, replicates, or larger hydration systems should be moved to a Linux workstation or VPS/HPC environment.
