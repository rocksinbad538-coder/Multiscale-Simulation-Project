## 4. Physical scope of the trajectory

The h-BN scaffold and all pyrene chromophores were frozen during the source MD trajectory.

Consequently, the time-dependent variation represents solvent-induced electrostatic fluctuations for fixed solute geometries:

\[
\delta E_i(t)
\approx
\delta E_i^{\mathrm{solvent}}(t)
\]

The trajectory does not contain chromophore geometric disorder, scaffold geometric disorder, time-dependent pyrene rotation or translation, or vibrational modulation of the chromophore geometries.

The mean energy differences between different chromophores may nevertheless contain static chromophore-geometry contributions, static site or orientation contributions, and solvent electrostatic contributions.

A useful conceptual decomposition is:

\[
E_i(t)
=
E_i^{\mathrm{geometry/static}}
+
\Delta E_i^{\mathrm{site}}
+
\delta E_i^{\mathrm{solvent}}(t)
\]

The current embedded calculations do not yet isolate these contributions.

## 5. Adiabatic-root analysis

The initial trajectory was constructed using the lowest adiabatic root, \(S_1\), for every chromophore.

This produced a formally valid lowest-root trajectory, but the TDDFT configuration analysis showed that the same root number did not correspond to the same electronic excitation for all four chromophores.

The original \(S_1\)-based products were therefore retained as diagnostic products and not adopted as the principal one-state-per-site excitonic representation.

---

## 6. Electronic-state identity

The low-lying bright local state was tracked between \(S_1\) and \(S_2\) using the weight of the `52a -> 53a` HOMO-to-LUMO configuration.

The root-ordering pattern remained unchanged across all 21 frames:

| Chromophore | Tracked root | Frames |
|---|---:|---:|
| PYR2 | S2 | 21 |
| PYR3 | S2 | 21 |
| PYR4 | S2 | 21 |
| PYR5 | S1 | 21 |

Thus:

\[
\text{PYR2–PYR4: tracked bright state}=S_2
\]

\[
\text{PYR5: tracked bright state}=S_1
\]

Root number alone is therefore not a valid universal state label for the four chromophores.

The tracked state was selected as:

\[
n_i(t)
=
\operatorname*{arg\,max}_{n\in\{1,2\}}
w_{52a\rightarrow53a}^{(n)}
\]

and the tracked site energy was defined as:

\[
E_i^{\mathrm{tracked}}(t)
=
E_{n_i(t)}(t)
\]

## 8. PYR5 site offset

The frame-dependent PYR5 offset was calculated relative to the mean energy of PYR2–PYR4:

\[
\Delta E_{\mathrm{PYR5}}(t)
=
\frac{
E_{\mathrm{PYR2}}(t)
+
E_{\mathrm{PYR3}}(t)
+
E_{\mathrm{PYR4}}(t)
}{3}
-
E_{\mathrm{PYR5}}(t)
\]

Results:

- Mean offset: 308.794 meV
- Standard deviation: 18.483 meV
- Minimum offset: 272.667 meV
- Maximum offset: 340.667 meV

The offset remained present over the full 100 ps sampling interval.

The PYR5 shift cannot yet be assigned exclusively to solvent electrostatics because PYR5 also shows a measurable static structural difference relative to PYR2–PYR4.

## 10. Hamiltonian products

Two diagonal representations were retained.

### 10.1 Lowest-adiabatic-root products

These products use \(S_1\) for every chromophore.

They describe the lowest adiabatic excitation energy at each site but do not form an electronically homogeneous local-state manifold because the electronic character of \(S_1\) differs between PYR2–PYR4 and PYR5.

### 10.2 Electronically tracked products

These products follow the bright HOMO-to-LUMO-dominated local excitation.

The absolute diagonal Hamiltonian is:

\[
H_{\mathrm{diag}}(t)
=
\operatorname{diag}
\left[
E_{\mathrm{PYR2}}(t),
E_{\mathrm{PYR3}}(t),
E_{\mathrm{PYR4}}(t),
E_{\mathrm{PYR5}}(t)
\right]
\]

Generated products:

- 21 absolute diagonal Hamiltonians in CSV format
- 21 absolute diagonal Hamiltonians in NPY format
- 21 per-frame-centered diagonal Hamiltonians in CSV format
- 21 per-frame-centered diagonal Hamiltonians in NPY format

The centered Hamiltonian is:

\[
H_{\mathrm{centered}}(t)
=
H_{\mathrm{diag}}(t)
-
\overline{E}(t)I
\]

where:

\[
\overline{E}(t)
=
\frac{1}{4}
\sum_{i=1}^{4}E_i(t)
\]

Subtracting the common identity contribution preserves population and coherence dynamics up to a global phase.

The centered Hamiltonians must not replace the absolute energies when calculating absolute optical transition positions.

Off-diagonal excitonic couplings \(J_{ij}\) are not included yet.

The complete production dataset supports the following conclusions:

1. The embedded TDDFT production is technically complete.
2. All 84 calculations passed the defined numerical and output-quality checks.
3. Root number cannot be used as a universal electronic-state label.
4. The same bright local excitation corresponds to \(S_2\) in PYR2–PYR4 and \(S_1\) in PYR5.
5. State tracking by configuration character is stable across all 21 frames.
6. PYR5 remains systematically lower in energy than PYR2–PYR4.
7. The mean tracked PYR5 offset is 308.794 meV.
8. The temporal fluctuations represent solvent electrostatic disorder under frozen-solute conditions.
9. The mean site offset may contain both static structural/site effects and solvent contributions.
10. The current Hamiltonians contain validated diagonal terms only.
11. Off-diagonal excitonic couplings remain to be parameterized.
12. A one-state-per-site model is plausible but requires further NTO and vacuum-reference validation.
13. A two-state-per-site model must remain available until the low-state manifold is fully characterized.
