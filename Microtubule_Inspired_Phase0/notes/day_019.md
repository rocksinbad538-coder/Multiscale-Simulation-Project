## 1. Objective

Day019 established, validated, and dynamically tested the bright-state excitonic model constructed from the accepted frozen-solute molecular-dynamics ensemble and the embedded TDDFT calculations.

The work addressed the following questions:

1. Whether the ORCA transition-density cubes reproduce the independently printed transition dipoles.
2. Whether the point-transition-dipole approximation is adequate at the present chromophore separations.
3. Whether finite-size transition-density corrections materially change the bright-state Hamiltonian.
4. Which dynamical analyses are supported by the current 21 embedded solvent snapshots.
5. How the bright-state dynamics respond to phenomenological pure dephasing.
6. Whether PYR5 is coherently accessible from the PYR2–PYR4 bright manifold.

## 4. Transition-density-derived atomic-charge coupling benchmark

A transition-density-derived atom-centered charge model, denoted TDC-AC, was constructed.

### 4.1 Procedure

1. Every transition-density voxel was assigned to its nearest atom through an atom-centered Voronoi partition.
2. The transition density was integrated into 26 atomic transition charges per chromophore.
3. A minimum-norm charge correction imposed exactly:
   - zero total transition charge;
   - the independently printed ORCA transition-dipole vector.
4. Unscreened Coulomb couplings were evaluated between the atomic transition charges.

The TDC-AC model is an atom-resolved finite-size coupling approximation.

It is not the exact continuous-density double integral:

$begin:math:display$
J\_\{ij\}
\=
\\iint
\\frac\{
\\rho\_i\^\{\\mathrm\{tr\}\}\(\\mathbf r\)
\\rho\_j\^\{\\mathrm\{tr\}\}\(\\mathbf r\'\)
\}\{
\|\\mathbf r\-\\mathbf r\'\|
\}
\\\,d\\mathbf r\\\,d\\mathbf r\'\.
$end:math:display$

## 5. Finite-size-corrected Hamiltonians

Six pair-specific correction factors were defined as:

$begin:math:display$
c\_\{ij\}
\=
\\frac\{
J\_\{ij\}\^\{\\mathrm\{TDC\-AC\}\}\(0\)
\}\{
J\_\{ij\}\^\{\\mathrm\{point\}\}\(0\)
\}\.
$end:math:display$

The factors were applied to the corresponding bright–bright point-dipole couplings in all 21 solvent snapshots:

$begin:math:display$
J\_\{ij\}\^\{\\mathrm\{corr\}\}\(t\)
\=
c\_\{ij\}
J\_\{ij\}\^\{\\mathrm\{point\}\}\(t\)\.
$end:math:display$

This transfer is consistent with:

- frozen chromophore geometries;
- stable bright transition-dipole directions;
- finite-size corrections dominated by the fixed spatial extent of the transition density.

It is not equivalent to calculating an embedded transition-density cube for every solvent frame.

### 5.1 Generated models

Two corrected models were generated:

1. **Four-state bright Hamiltonian**
   - primary finite-size-corrected model;
   - basis:
     - PYR2 bright;
     - PYR3 bright;
     - PYR4 bright;
     - PYR5 bright.

2. **Full eight-state hybrid Hamiltonian**
   - sensitivity model only;
   - bright–bright couplings corrected;
   - alternate and mixed-family couplings retained from the point-dipole model.

### 5.2 Dynamic sensitivity across 21 frames

- Hamiltonian snapshots: 21/21.
- Maximum absolute coupling correction: 0.067962 meV.
- Maximum full-eight-state eigenvalue shift: 0.042075 meV.
- Maximum four-state bright eigenvalue shift: 0.042074 meV.
- Minimum matched bright eigenvector overlap: 0.9998556977.
- Maximum correction / maximum diagonal-energy SD: 0.004239.
- Maximum correction / minimum local S1–S2 gap: 0.001282.

### 5.3 Accepted conclusion

The point-dipole and TDC-AC-corrected bright models remain in the same physical regime.

Finite-size corrections can accumulate coherent phase differences over long propagation times, but they do not materially change:

- energy ordering;
- eigenstate identity;
- localization regime;
- weak-coupling character;
- dominance of diagonal disorder.

The four-state TDC-AC-corrected bright Hamiltonian is accepted as the primary corrected model.

## 8. Haken–Strobl pure-dephasing sensitivity

A phenomenological pure-dephasing sensitivity analysis was performed using the four-state TDC-AC-corrected bright Hamiltonian.

### 8.1 Model definition

For every local bright state $begin:math:text$i$end:math:text$, the Lindblad operator was:

$begin:math:display$
L\_i
\=
\\sqrt\{\\gamma\_\\phi\}
\|i\\rangle\\langle i\|\.
$end:math:display$

With this convention, every off-diagonal density-matrix element decays directly at $begin:math:text$\\gamma\_\\phi$end:math:text$ in the absence of Hamiltonian evolution.

The calculation did not include:

- population-relaxation operators;
- thermal detailed balance;
- a solvent spectral density;
- microscopic dephasing-rate parameterization.

### 8.2 Dephasing sweep

$begin:math:display$
\\gamma\_\\phi
\=

$end:math:display$

Equivalent nominal dephasing times:

$begin:math:display$
T\_\\phi

\\gamma\_\\phi\^\{\-1\}\.

Within the tested interval:

- increasing pure dephasing suppresses $begin:math:text$l\_1$end:math:text$ coherence;
- population redistribution within PYR2–PYR4 increases monotonically;
- the turnover toward a high-dephasing or quantum-Zeno regime was not reached;
- the largest tested redistribution occurs at the upper boundary:
  $begin:math:display$

The Haken–Strobl model:

- does not enforce detailed balance;
- does not define a thermal equilibrium state;
- cannot provide a microscopic PYR2–PYR4 to PYR5 relaxation mechanism.
