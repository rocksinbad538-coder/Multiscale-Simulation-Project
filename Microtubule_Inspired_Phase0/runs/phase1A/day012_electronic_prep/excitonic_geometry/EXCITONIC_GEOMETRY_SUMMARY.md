# Phase 1A Pyrene Excitonic Geometry Summary

Input structure:

- Accepted hydrated Phase 1A baseline
- h-BN bonded scaffold, kang2000 + improperGeo100
- Hydrated and post-minimized reference structure

## Pyrene orientation pattern

The four pyrene chromophores form an alternating orientation pattern around the nanotube.

Neighboring chromophores are nearly orthogonal:

- PYR2–PYR3: 89.997°
- PYR3–PYR4: 89.923°
- PYR4–PYR5: 89.460°

Non-neighboring same-orientation pairs are nearly parallel:

- PYR2–PYR4: 1.149°
- PYR3–PYR5: 0.993°

## Interpretation

This is not a close pi-stacked aggregate. The chromophores form an alternating, quasi-orthogonal arrangement.

The nearest pairs are spatially closer but nearly orthogonal, while the parallel pairs are farther apart and laterally displaced.

Expected consequence:

- Direct excitonic coupling may be weak to moderate.
- Coupling may be anisotropic and pair-dependent.
- PYR2–PYR4 and PYR3–PYR5 are the most relevant same-orientation pairs.
- PYR2–PYR3, PYR3–PYR4, and PYR4–PYR5 are the nearest-neighbor pairs but with near-orthogonal transition-plane geometry.

This geometry should be used to guide the first TDDFT/site-energy and coupling calculations.
