# Hydrated Hamiltonian Sensitivity Interpretation

## Objective

Assess the impact of replacing the vacuum-derived PYR4-PYR5 coupling estimate
(J45 ≈ 11 meV) with the hydrated bright-state estimate
(J45 ≈ 33 meV).

## Main observation

The hydrated value does not merely perturb the Hamiltonian.

Increasing J45 from 11 meV to 33 meV produces:

- stronger energetic separation of the excitonic manifold,
- increased PYR4-PYR5 hybridization,
- increased localization of the PYR2/PYR3-dominated states,
- significant redistribution of excitonic weights.

## Quantitative changes

### Lowest exciton

X1:

- 3.738 eV → 3.724 eV
- shift ≈ -14.5 meV

### Highest exciton

X4:

- 3.789 eV → 3.803 eV
- shift ≈ +14.5 meV

### Total excitonic bandwidth

Baseline:

- X4 − X1 = 50.5 meV

Hydrated estimate:

- X4 − X1 = 79.6 meV

Increase:

- ≈ 58 %

## Eigenstate reorganization

Baseline:

X1:
- PYR5 = 68 %

X4:
- PYR4 = 69 %

Hydrated:

X1:
- PYR5 = 68 %
- PYR4 contribution increases substantially

X4:
- PYR4 = 66 %
- PYR5 contribution increases substantially

Therefore the hydrated coupling strengthens the PYR4-PYR5 excitonic subspace.

## Physical interpretation

Explicit local hydration introduces two distinct effects:

1. A dark inter-chromophore charge-transfer-like state
   (S1 at 2.959 eV).

2. A larger effective bright-state splitting between
   Frenkel-like excitations.

The second effect increases the effective PYR4-PYR5 coupling
and reorganizes the four-site excitonic Hamiltonian.

## Recommendation

For Phase 1A:

- retain the vacuum Hamiltonian as the primary reference model;
- retain the hydrated J45 ≈ 33 meV value as a sensitivity-tested refinement;
- do not replace the vacuum Hamiltonian globally until additional hydrated dimers have been characterized.

Current status:

- vacuum model validated;
- hydrated correction identified;
- hydrated correction shown to have non-negligible Hamiltonian-level consequences.
